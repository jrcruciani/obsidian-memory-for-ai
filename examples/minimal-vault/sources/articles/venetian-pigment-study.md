# New Findings on Venetian Pigment Degradation

A 2026 study by researchers at the Courtauld Institute analyzed micro-samples from 47 Venetian Renaissance paintings, finding that lead-tin yellow degrades faster than previously thought when exposed to UV cleaning methods. The study recommends limiting UV exposure during restoration to under 200 hours cumulative.

Key findings:
- Lead-tin yellow shows measurable degradation at 150 hours UV exposure
- Verdigris is more stable than expected under controlled humidity
- The combination of lead white + ultramarine creates a self-stabilizing matrix
- Recommended protocol: spectral analysis before any UV-based cleaning

The authors note this contradicts the 2019 guidelines from the European Conservators Association, which set the UV threshold at 500 hours.
